What is the Indus water treaty and why is it important?

what is it?


The Indus Waters Treaty, signed in 1960 between India and Pakistan and brokered by the World Bank, is a water-sharing agreement concerning the Indus River system. It allocates the three eastern rivers (Ravi, Beas, and Sutlej) to India and the three western rivers (Indus, Jhelum, and Chenab) to Pakistan. Despite tensions between the two nations, the treaty has largely endured, serving as a key example of successful transboundary water management. It also outlines mechanisms for cooperation, data sharing, and conflict resolution related to water use.
